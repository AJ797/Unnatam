\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented two composable architectural modifications for hybrid state-space and attention language models. \emph{Intracellular attention} is a structurally minimal change inside the SSM block that allows the otherwise-independent state slots to interact via content-dependent attention before each readout. \emph{Hormone routing} extracts behavioural direction vectors from a partially-trained base model via document-style contrast pairs and re-injects a learned token-conditional combination of those frozen directions into the residual stream during pretraining. Both mechanisms add fewer than $1\%$ of trainable parameters, are gated such that the unmodified hybrid backbone is recovered exactly at initialisation, and compose without interference.

\paragraph{Future directions.} A fused CUDA kernel for intracellular attention would close the gap to the standard Mamba scan and remove the only practical barrier to applying the mechanism at scale. The hormone-extraction protocol, currently performed once at $1$B tokens, could be made continuous --- updating $V$ on a slow schedule throughout training --- which would let the bank track the model's evolving representations. Finally, hormone routing's central premise --- that a model's own emerging behavioural axes can be re-injected into its training signal --- suggests a broader research programme on \emph{introspective} architectural modifications that we have only begun to explore.
